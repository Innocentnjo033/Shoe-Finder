Brands

Nike

Nike, established in 1964 by Bill Bowerman and Phil Knight as Blue Ribbon Sports and later rebranded as Nike in 1971, has emerged as a dominant force in athletic footwear and apparel globally. Throughout the late '70s and early '80s, the brand soared to prominence through groundbreaking designs such as the Waffle Trainer and innovative Air technology, which revolutionised the athletic shoe industry. In 1984, Nike embarked on a landmark partnership with basketball icon Michael Jordan, leading to the creation of the Air Jordan line. This collaboration transcended the basketball court, transforming sneaker culture and cementing the Air Jordan series as a symbol of unparalleled style and performance, remaining immensely sought-after and influential in the world of trainers.

The name Nike has a historical lineage that spans over 3,000 years, originating from Ancient Greece to Rome and eventually becoming associated with an American sportswear brand. In Ancient Greece, Nike symbolised strength, swiftness, and triumph, making her an ideal embodiment for a brand focused on sportswear.

Adidas

Adidas, established in 1949 by Adolf Dassler, boasts a rich history of impactful collaborations that have shaped its brand legacy. One noteworthy partnership began in 2015 with the renowned rapper Kanye West. This collaboration birthed the Adidas Yeezy line, merging high fashion with athletic footwear, and gained widespread acclaim for its cutting-edge designs and limited releases. Kanye West's artistic influence coupled with Adidas' technical expertise propelled the Yeezy line to the forefront of sneaker culture, earning global acclaim.

However, in 2021, Adidas and Kanye West, now known as Ye, parted ways, marking the end of their collaboration. While specific reasons for the split were not extensively disclosed, it is believed that divergent visions and strategies played a role in the decision. Despite the conclusion of this influential partnership, Adidas remains committed to collaborating with various artists and personalities. Notably, the brand collaborated with Bad Bunny, the Puerto Rican reggaeton artist, resulting in distinctive sneaker releases and clothing collections that merged Bad Bunny's unique style with Adidas' sporting heritage, showcasing the brand's dedication to collaborations and innovation post the Kanye West era.

The 3 most iconic Adidas shoes are:

1. Adidas Superstar: Originally launched in 1969, these sneakers have become an enduring symbol in sneaker culture. The Superstar boasts a timeless design, featuring its distinctive rubber shell toe and leather upper, retaining a prominent place in streetwear fashion.

2. Adidas Stan Smith: Initially designed as a tennis shoe in the 1960s, the Stan Smith has transcended its sporty origins to become a fashion icon. Known for its clean and simplistic design, the shoe showcases a straightforward silhouette with perforated three-stripes and Stan Smith's portrait on the tongue.

3. Adidas Ultra Boost: Renowned for its remarkable comfort and responsive cushioning, the Ultra Boost gained popularity as a premium running shoe. It incorporates Adidas' pioneering Boost technology, offering a luxurious and energy-returning experience, making it a preferred choice among runners and sneaker enthusiasts alike.

Puma

Puma, established in 1948 by Rudolf Dassler in Germany, has a rich history rooted in athletic footwear and sportswear innovation. Initially founded as a split from the Dassler brothers' partnership (which also led to the creation of Adidas), Puma quickly gained recognition for its focus on performance and style. Over the years, the brand has expanded its offerings to encompass a diverse range of sports and lifestyle footwear. One of its most iconic shoes is the Puma Suede, introduced in 1968. The Puma Suede became an emblem of sneaker culture, known for its sleek silhouette, comfortable design, and association with basketball and hip-hop. Its timeless appeal and versatility have solidified its status as a classic in the sneaker world, remaining a staple in streetwear fashion.

New Balance

New Balance, established in 1906 in Boston, Massachusetts, holds a storied history as a reputable footwear company renowned for its dedication to comfort, functionality, and performance. Initially crafting arch supports and orthopaedic shoes, the brand transitioned to manufacturing athletic footwear by the mid-20th century. New Balance's commitment to quality craftsmanship and innovation has garnered a loyal following among athletes and casual wearers alike. Among its most coveted shoes are the New Balance 990 series, celebrated for its superior cushioning and stability, embodying the brand's dedication to craftsmanship. Additionally, the New Balance 574 stands as an iconic sneaker, recognized for its timeless design and versatility. The Fresh Foam series, particularly the Fresh Foam 1080, represents another popular choice, lauded for its plush cushioning and responsiveness, catering to runners seeking comfort and performance. These three models epitomize New Balance's legacy of comfort and style, cementing its position as a prominent player in the footwear industry.

Vans

Vans, established in California in 1966, has a deep-rooted history linked to skateboarding and urban fashion. Co-founded by Paul Van Doren, James Van Doren, Gordon C. Lee, and Serge D'Elia, the brand swiftly became intertwined with the skateboarding scene. Vans gained recognition for its robust yet stylish footwear, tailored specifically to meet skateboarders' demands for resilience, traction, and feel on the board. The Vans Era, introduced in 1976, emerged as an essential skate shoe recognized for its slim design and cushioned collar, perfectly meeting skateboarders' requirements. Another iconic shoe, the Vans Old Skool, released in 1977, is acclaimed for its durable materials, including suede and canvas, featuring the brand's distinctive side stripe that epitomizes the classic Vans aesthetic. Additionally, the Vans Authentic, a model existing since the brand's inception, is praised for its simplicity and enduring style, showcasing a low-profile design and sturdy canvas upper. These three shoe models symbolize Vans' enduring impact in skate culture and its talent in creating footwear that appeals to both skaters and fashion enthusiasts.
